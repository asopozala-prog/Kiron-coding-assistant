# Topic: Current Workplace



## Summary

Alex works as a Junior Legal Assistant in a law firm, where he supports supervising lawyers through document preparation, organization, and case administration. His role is essential to the firm's daily operations, even though it remains largely behind the scenes.

## Key facts

- Position: Junior Legal Assistant.
- Professional experience: Four years.
- Works in a legal office environment.
- Reports to supervising lawyers.
- Supports multiple legal professionals.
- Handles confidential legal documentation.
- Works primarily at a computer.
- Maintains organized digital and physical case files.
- Collaborates with administrative staff and lawyers.
- Rarely interacts directly with clients.

## Narrative

Alex's workplace is structured, detail-oriented, and built around confidentiality. Every document, email, and case file has legal significance, making accuracy more important than speed.

Although he is not responsible for legal decisions, his organizational work enables lawyers to access the right information when they need it. He takes pride in creating reliable systems that reduce friction throughout the legal process.

Alex appreciates the stability of legal administration but also recognizes that much of his daily work consists of repetitive processes that could be automated without reducing quality.

# Topic: Daily Workflow



## Summary

Alex follows a structured daily workflow built around receiving, organizing, verifying, and preparing legal documents. Much of his day involves repetitive but essential administrative work.

- Downloads legal documents.

# Topic: AI Restrictions



## Summary

Alex believes modern AI could significantly improve his productivity, but he cannot use cloud-based AI services because they conflict with his firm's confidentiality requirements. This limitation is the primary motivation for pursuing a private, locally hosted AI assistant.

## Key facts

- Cannot upload client data to cloud AI.
- Respects firm security policies.
- Aware of GDPR obligations.
- Understands attorney-client privilege.
- Wants local AI processing.
- Values complete control over sensitive information.
- Sees automation as a tool for reclaiming time rather than replacing people.
- Supports responsible AI adoption.

## Narrative

Alex has personally experimented with modern language models outside work and recognizes how effectively they can automate repetitive administrative tasks.

However, he also understands why these services cannot be used inside his workplace. Uploading confidential legal documents to external servers would violate both organizational policy and his own ethical standards.

Rather than viewing this limitation as frustrating, Alex sees it as a design challenge. His goal is not unrestricted AI access but a secure, locally hosted assistant that operates entirely within his own trusted environment.

For Alex, the purpose of automation is deeply personal. Every repetitive task delegated to a private AI system represents more time and mental energy available for reading, friendships, dating, and building the balanced life he hopes to achieve.
